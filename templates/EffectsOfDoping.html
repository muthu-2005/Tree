Health Effects of Doping

Understanding the consequences of doping on the human body


Prohibited Drugs

1. Cardiovascular Effects

Doping substances, like anabolic steroids and EPO, severely affect the cardiovascular system. These drugs can lead to thickened blood, increasing the risk of heart attacks, strokes, and sudden cardiac death.

2. Liver Damage

Steroids and oral enhancers heavily tax the liver, leading to liver failure or cancer in severe cases. Prolonged use of these substances stresses the liver, leading to liver toxicity, an increased risk of liver tumors and cancer, jaundice, and cholestasis (disruption of bile flow). The liver's ability to filter toxins is compromised, and this damage can result in long-term health complications, including liver failure. These risks underline the dangers of doping for individuals seeking to enhance performance.

3. Muscle damage

While steroids may promote rapid muscle growth initially, they lead to muscle atrophy once discontinued, as the body adapts to artificial hormonal support. The imbalance between muscle growth and the strength of tendons and ligaments increases the risk of injuries, including tendon damage and muscle ruptures. Steroids also disrupt natural hormonal production, impairing muscle recovery and repair, which can lead to chronic fatigue and delayed healing.

4. Neurological Damage

Stimulants and steroids can cause neurotoxicity, mood swings, and permanent cognitive impairments. These substances are known to induce depression, aggression, and even psychosis.
